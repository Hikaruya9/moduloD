Keith David is one of those guys where it's like, he should have gotten 45 extra opportunities in the 80s but just didn't. His first big break is The Thing and then it's a full four years before he even appears in another film. I look at him in The Thing and it's like Ripley in Alien; the unknown but compelling newcomer that outlives the bloodshed to the end credits. In a sane world that role catapults him to Sigourney-level stardom. But it didn't happen. And I think he is perfect in They Live playing off Roddy. They are a dynamic duo that leave you craving more camaraderie than there already is.
